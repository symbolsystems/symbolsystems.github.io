Use no more than one level of dereferencing [CODSTA-87-3]

DESCRIPTION 

"The use of pointers should be restricted. Specifically, no more than one level
of dereferencing is allowed."
SINCE 

v7.3
BENEFITS 

"Pointers are easily misused, even by experienced programmers. They can make it
hard to follow or analyze the flow of data in a program, especially by tool-based
static analyzers."
EXAMPLE 

void foo(){
    int** p;
    int** q;
    int i;

    **p = 1; // Violation
    i = **q; // Violation
}
REPAIR 

Do not use more than one level of dereferencing.
REFERENCES 

The Power of Ten - Rules for Developing Safety Critical Code.
Rule 9
